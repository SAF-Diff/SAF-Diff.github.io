SAF-Diff: Skill-Aware Flow-Diffusion Model for Robotic Manipulation

Code

Abstract

Skill transfer in robotic manipulation is critical to enabling robots to learn and adapt tasks from prior experiences or demonstrations, especially in complex and dynamic environments. However, current approaches face significant limitations, including poor robustness in unfamiliar scenarios, insufficient scalability for diverse and dynamic tasks, and inefficiencies from extensive data requirements. In this work, we propose a new method for efficient and robust skill transfer that leverages skill-aware tokens for representation learning and conditions a diffusion process to generate task-consistent motion flows. To enhance precision and adaptability, we introduce a nonlinear optimization-based strategy that merges multiview flows and maps them into 3D space without additional training. To evaluate our method, we constructed a high-fidelity, skill-aware dataset on the Isaac Lab platform featuring diverse and complex scenarios. Experimental results in both simulation and real-world environments demonstrate superior generalization and state-of-the-art performance in skill transfer tasks.
